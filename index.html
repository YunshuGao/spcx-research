Bull vs bear

Bull case

  • Starlink: only global LEO broadband at scale, profitable (+$4.4B), +50% growth
  • ~80% of world's mass to orbit; Starship reusability optionality
  • Frontier AI (Grok) + ≈$90B cash war chest
  • Oppenheimer: outperform, $190 target (~+18%)

Bear case

  • ≈95× sales, loss-making (−$4.9B FY25); AI burned $12.7B capex
  • Starlink ARPU falling: $99 → $91 → $81
  • Musk 82.4% votes, part-time, no key-man insurance
  • Lock-up cascade over ~12 months; Chanos calls it "hopes and dreams"

How to buy from Sydney

A$10,000 ≈ US$6,500 ≈ ~40 shares at $161 (check the live AUD/USD rate).

  1. Open an ASIC-regulated broker — IBKR (cheapest FX), Stake or moomoo
  2. Sign the W-8BEN form and add your TFN
  3. Convert A$ → US$ — the FX spread is the real cost on $10k
  4. Buy ticker SPCX with a limit order (never market); consider staggering your entry
  5. Settles T+1, held via a US custodian — keep contract notes for the ATO

Key risks (from the prospectus)

  • Starship delays/failures — the #1 listed risk; the whole growth story depends on it
  • Key-man: total dependence on Musk; he doesn't work full-time; no key-person insurance
  • Governance: Class A holders have ~no power; charter renounces corporate opportunities; mandatory arbitration
  • Related parties: undefined-terms Tesla deals (Terafab, Macrohard); $60B Cursor option
  • Competition: Amazon Kuiper, Blue Origin, Chinese constellations; OpenAI/Google in AI
  • Unproven bets: orbital AI compute (2028+), lunar economy, Mars — priced in today
  • Currency: unhedged AUD/USD — a rising AUD erodes your return even if SPCX rises
